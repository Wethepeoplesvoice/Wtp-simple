Turn your summary into drafts

First, use the ‚ÄúCivil rights & constitutional concerns‚Äù wizard to build a summary. Then use these tools to turn that summary into a draft judicial complaint-style letter, a motion to strike / amend, or a supporting affidavit you can edit and use with a lawyer or court forms.

Judicial Complaint Draft

Uses your summary to create a structured complaint-style letter you can adapt for a judicial oversight body.

This is a starting point only. Check the actual complaint form/instructions for the judicial oversight body where you plan to file.

Motion to Strike / Amend Draft

Wraps your summary into a motion-style format asking a court to correct or strike false or unsupported statements.

You will still need to format this into your court‚Äôs official caption, case number, signature block, and follow local rules.

Supporting Affidavit Draft

Uses your summary as sworn statement language that you can adapt into an affidavit and have notarized, if appropriate.

Affidavit requirements vary by state. Treat this as draft language to refine with an attorney or using your court‚Äôs affidavit form.
