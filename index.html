WHY THIS MATTERS

A PEACEFUL MOVEMENT FOR ACCOUNTABILITY

Government has grown bigger, more complicated, and harder to follow. Most people are working, raising families, and trying to survive — they don’t have hours to dig through bills, budgets, and legal language. That gap is where bad decisions, quiet deals, and unfair policies slip through.

THE GAP
Too Busy to Watch
While most Americans are working, caring for kids, or taking care of loved ones, major decisions are made in long hearings and quiet committee rooms. The people paying the bills rarely see what’s happening in time to respond.
THE ANSWER
One Watchlist, Millions of Eyes
America’s Watchlist turns scattered data into clear alerts: what changed, who did it, and how it hits regular people. No parties, no drama, no violence — just facts and peaceful pressure from citizens who are paying attention.

NATIONAL VIEW
Patterns of Power
Citizens can see national trends: which issues keep repeating, which states are quietly passing copy-cat bills, and where the same special interests appear again and again.
  • Track repeat “sneaky riders” in multiple states.

NATIONAL & STATE TOWN HALLS

VOICE-ONLY STREAMS · NO CAMERA NEEDED

In a full launch, America’s Watchlist would host national briefings and state-level town halls whenever major alerts cross a threshold — using AI voice-over and visuals so you never have to be on camera.

NATIONAL BRIEFINGS
Weekly People’s Update
A recurring national stream breaking down “what happened this week” with bills, spending, and influence. No party spin — just straight talk and visuals citizens can actually understand.
STATE TOWN HALLS
Triggered by Alerts
When a state crosses a red-flag threshold — like a big tax hike or a suspicious bill — supporters in that state get invited to a live, voice-only town hall to go deeper and plan peaceful action.
