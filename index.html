Why This Matters

For Everyone Tired of Being Ignored

A lot of us feel the same thing, but we’re spread out and alone with it: anger, fear for our kids’ future, and the feeling that the people in Washington care more about TV cameras and donors than the people who actually sent them there.

Most Americans are working, caring for family, and just trying to get through the week. We don’t have time to dig through legal pages and government websites to figure out who raised our bills, who protected their friends, or who quietly buried the truth.

Meanwhile, billionaires, big corporations, and professional lobbyists are in the room every single day. They know every vote, every loophole, every back door. They can pour money into the system until the same politicians stay in power for decades and grow richer while the country grows more divided.

Somewhere along the way, too many leaders forgot who they work for. They forgot the people back home who are paying higher prices, watching their rights and savings get chipped away, and wondering if anyone in power actually sees them anymore.

America’s Watchlist exists for the people who are done just complaining. It’s for the moms, dads, grandparents, and young people who still love this country, who still believe in peaceful change, and who know deep down that if we don’t stand together now, we may wake up one day and realize we waited too long.

This platform is a simple promise: to shine light on what they’re doing, in language everyone can understand, and give millions of ordinary Americans an easy way to act together instead of shouting alone at a screen.

What America’s Watchlist Could Flag

These are example alerts to show how the platform could look. They’re not live data yet — but this is how “The People’s Eyes on Power” might work once real bill tracking and finance APIs are connected.

Power & Utilities

Red-Flag Rate Hike

Georgia · 12% rate increase tied to fuel surcharges and new fees. Alerts explain who voted for it, what it pays for, and how much the average bill goes up.

Cost of Living

Property Tax & Local Spending

South Carolina · New reassessments plus local projects that raise taxes. Citizens can see where the money is going and who pushed it through.

Sneaky Bills

Hidden Riders in a “Small” Bill

Federal · Harmless-sounding bill with pages of add-ons and quiet favors. The platform highlights the buried language and links it to donors and lobbyists.

Ethics & Trust

When Officials Cross the Line

Alerts trigger when an official is formally reprimanded, investigated, or tied to serious misconduct — and show citizens exactly how to respond peacefully but firmly.

National & State Town Halls

Voice-Only Streams, No Camera Needed

In a full launch, America’s Watchlist would host national briefings and state-level town halls whenever major alerts cross a threshold — using voice-over and visuals so citizens never have to be on camera.

National Briefings

Weekly People’s Update

A recurring national stream breaking down “what happened this week” with bills, spending, and influence. No drama, no party spin — just straight talk and clear visuals.

State Town Halls

Triggered by Alerts

When a state crosses a red-flag threshold — like a big tax hike or a suspicious bill — supporters in that state can be invited to a live, voice-only town hall to go deeper and plan peaceful action.

National Petitions – America’s Watchlist

We’re Done Being Ignored

These petitions are written for everyday Americans — not lobbyists, not party insiders. They focus on the same core problem: a government that has drifted away from the people who are supposed to be in charge.

Petition #1

Real Transparency and Accountability in Government

To the elected officials of the United States:

We, the undersigned citizens, are tired of feeling lied to, kept in the dark, and talked down to by the very people who are supposed to work for us.

We are not asking for special treatment. We are asking for basic honesty and accountability from our government:

1. Real-time transparency for major federal spending, taxes, and utility rate hikes that hit families directly — in plain language that ordinary people can actually understand.

2. Full disclosure of large donations, financial conflicts, and foreign-linked income for all members of Congress and senior officials, with easy, public tools for citizens to see who is paying whom.

3. Stronger, faster oversight when officials abuse their office, break the public trust, or protect their friends instead of the law — with real consequences, not just speeches and finger-pointing.

4. Protection for whistleblowers and truth-tellers who expose corruption, abuse, and hidden influence — without fear of losing their jobs or being destroyed for telling the truth.

We believe these demands are not left or right. They are American. Without honest information and real accountability, there is no way for the people to truly be in charge.

We call on you to support meaningful legislation and rules that make our government transparent, accountable, and worthy of the trust it has lost.

Petition #2

6-Year Term Limits for Congress

To the United States Congress:

We, the undersigned, believe that public office is a temporary duty, not a lifetime career.

For too long, the same politicians have stayed in office for 10, 20, even 30+ years, building power, donors, and influence while trust in government collapses. This is not what representation was meant to be.

We call for a firm limit of 6 years total in Congress for any individual:

• Up to three terms in the House (3 × 2 years)
• Or one term in the Senate (1 × 6 years)
• Any combination totaling 6 years and out

No grandfathering, no special carve-outs, no “seniority” loopholes. When your time is finished, you step aside and let new voices serve.

As long as the same people stay in power for decades, backed by big donors and party machines, the American people will never fully trust that government belongs to them again.

We demand that you support and advance a serious, enforceable term-limits amendment or legislation reflecting these principles.

I support 6-year total term limits for Congress and want this message used to push for that change.
