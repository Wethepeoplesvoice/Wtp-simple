Live Bill Alerts · Prototype

plain-english bill breakdowns

These are demo examples of how America’s Torch would explain real bills: what they do, who pays, who benefits, and what looks crazy. In a future version, this section will pull from live data for Congress and state legislatures.

STATUS · PASSED HOUSE · HEADING TO SENATE
Example · Utility Fees

“Utility Grid Stabilization Act”

What it really does: Lets power companies add a new “grid stability” fee to every monthly bill — even if your usage goes down — to cover “infrastructure upgrades”.

Who benefits: Large utility companies and investors.
Who pays: Every household and small business customer.

What looks crazy: The bill weakens the refund rules if companies overcharge you and makes it harder for citizens to contest rate hikes at public hearings.

Prototype only – this is a sample alert showing the style of breakdown America’s Torch will provide.

STATUS · COMMITTEE STAGE · RUSHED HEARING
Example · Property Taxes

“Local Revenue Modernization Bill”

What it really does: Allows counties to re-assess property values more often, which can push taxes up faster even when your income stays the same.

Who benefits: Local governments with budget gaps.
Who pays: Homeowners, especially on fixed incomes.

What looks crazy: A hidden clause reduces notice time before increases and schedules public hearings in the middle of the workday, making it harder for regular people to show up and object.

In a live system, this card would link to your county, your reps, and peaceful actions you can take.

STATUS · SIGNED INTO LAW · TAKES EFFECT NEXT YEAR
Example · Hidden Riders

“Critical Infrastructure Security Act”

What it really does: The public title focuses on “security”, but a buried section relaxes reporting requirements for certain large contractors and shields some records from open-records requests.

Who benefits: Specific contractors and agencies with less transparency.
Who loses: Citizens, watchdogs, and local journalists trying to follow the money.

What looks crazy: The “security” bill was voted on just before a holiday weekend, and the rider language was added at the last minute with no separate debate.

Future versions of America’s Torch will track midnight votes, last-minute riders, and who sponsored them – with exportable timelines for oversight and media.
