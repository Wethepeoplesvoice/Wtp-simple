Government has grown bigger, more complicated, and harder to follow. Most people are working, raising families, and trying to survive — they don’t have hours to dig through bills, budgets, and legal language. That gap is where bad decisions, quiet deals, and unfair policies slip through.

Every year, the IRS collects trillions of dollars from families, workers, and small businesses. States add on their own income taxes, sales taxes, property taxes, gas taxes, “fees,” and fines. But almost nobody can see, in plain English, where that money actually goes — or how much is wasted on bloated agencies, useless positions, no-cap contracts, and projects the American people never truly agreed to.

While families cut coupons and choose between groceries and medicine, the federal and state governments rarely cut themselves. Instead, they add new departments, hire for jobs that don’t need to exist, and sign open-ended contracts where private companies drain tax dollars with almost no oversight or spending caps.

Somewhere along the way, too many leaders forgot who they work for. They forgot the people back home who are paying higher prices, watching their rights and savings get chipped away, and wondering if anyone in power actually sees them anymore.

America’s Watchlist exists for the people who are done just complaining. It’s for the moms, dads, grandparents, and young people who still love this country, who still believe in peaceful change, and who know deep down that if we don’t stand together now, we may wake up one day and realize we waited too long.

This platform is a simple promise: to shine light on what they’re doing, in language everyone can understand, and give millions of ordinary Americans an easy way to act together instead of shouting alone at a screen.

TAKE ACTION

A Peaceful Movement for Accountability

America’s Watchlist is not another place to argue online. It’s a set of tools for real-world, peaceful pressure: citizens seeing the same facts at the same time and moving together.

1. See What’s Really Happening

Live dashboards can track bills, votes, rate hikes, and spending in plain English — so you can see when something dangerous is moving before it’s too late to react.

2. Vote on Proposals & Petitions

Citizens can vote on national proposals, sign focused petitions, and help shape which problems we push on first — instead of waiting for party leaders or cable news to tell us what matters.

3. Send Real Complaints, Not Just Posts

When officials cross the line, the platform can help citizens fill out official ethics, oversight, or civil-rights forms and send them to the right committee — federal or state — instead of hunting for links one by one.

4. Pressure Without Violence

No riots, no threats, no doxxing. Just millions of citizens watching the same alerts, signing the same petitions, and sending clear, peaceful messages that can’t be ignored.

Follow Your Money

Tax, Waste, & the Cost of a Bloated Government

Every year, the IRS and the states collect enormous amounts of tax money from ordinary people. What almost nobody sees is how much of that money disappears into wasteful programs, duplicated agencies, unnecessary jobs, and contractor deals with no real caps.

Where the Money Goes (and Disappears)

A real “Follow Your Money” dashboard would show how federal and state budgets are actually spent — and where they’re wasted:

  • Agencies that grow every year while services get worse.
  • Departments doing the same job as another department.
  • Government jobs that exist to protect budgets, not serve people.
  • “Use it or lose it” spending sprees at the end of the fiscal year.

No-Cap Contractors & Endless Projects

Too often, private companies get open-ended contracts with no real spending caps, weak oversight, and no meaningful punishment when projects run over budget.

  • Contracts that double or triple their original cost.
  • Projects that never finish but keep getting funded.
  • Consulting deals that produce reports, not results.

Petition #1

Real Transparency and Accountability in Government

To the elected officials of the United States:

We, the undersigned citizens, are deeply concerned about the growing distance between government power and the people it is supposed to serve. We call for:

  1. Real-time transparency for major federal spending, rate hikes, and new taxes that impact households.
  2. Full disclosure of large donations, financial conflicts, and foreign-linked income for all members of Congress and senior officials, with easy, public tools for citizens to see who is paying whom.
  3. Stronger, faster oversight when officials abuse their office, break the public trust, ignore the will of the people, or protect their friends instead of the law.

These reforms are not about left or right — they are about restoring basic trust, honesty, and accountability in a government that is supposed to belong to the people.

Petition #2

6-Year Term Limits for Congress

To the United States Congress:

We, the undersigned, believe that public office is a temporary duty, not a lifetime career. When the same politicians stay in power for decades, they become more loyal to donors, lobbyists, and their own status than to the people back home.

We call for a national law that:

  1. Limits members of the House to a total of three terms (6 years).
  2. Limits members of the Senate to a total of one term (6 years).
  3. Prevents any member from using loopholes, appointments, or “musical chairs” jobs to stay in power indefinitely.

Without real term limits, the American people will never fully trust the federal government again. Power will never return to ordinary citizens as long as the same insiders stay in office for life, fueled by big donors and billionaire money.

Demo only: like Petition #1, signatures are stored in your Formspree inbox for now so you can track and export them. In a future build, they can be verified and forwarded automatically to Congress.
Petition #3

Follow the Money & Tax Waste Transparency Act

To the Congress and state legislatures of the United States:

We, the undersigned, are demanding a new era of honesty about how our tax dollars are collected, spent, and wasted. We call for laws that:

  1. Require a public, easy-to-read breakdown of federal, state, and local spending that any citizen can understand without a law degree.
  2. Publish annual waste reports for every major agency, including unused funds, duplicate programs, and bloated departments that do not serve the public.
  3. Automatically return unused or unnecessary funds to taxpayers or apply them to paying down national and state debt — not new pet projects.

For too long, Americans have been told to “just trust” that their money is being used wisely. We no longer accept that. If you are proud of how you use our money, you should have no problem showing it in plain English.

Demo only: this petition also goes to your Formspree inbox for counting and export.
Petition #4

Stop Useless Government Jobs & No-Cap Contractor Deals

To federal and state leaders:

We are tired of watching government grow on paper while nothing improves in our daily lives. We are tired of:

  1. Government jobs that exist mainly to protect budgets and titles, not to serve the American people.
  2. Bloated agencies with multiple departments doing the same work, while taxpayers pay the bill.
  3. Contractor deals with no meaningful spending caps, weak oversight, and no real consequences when projects go over budget or never deliver.

We demand laws that cap contractor spending, require competitive bidding, and force agencies to justify every new job and every new department in plain English. If a job or contract doesn’t clearly serve the people, it should not be funded with our money.

Demo only: as with the other petitions, signatures go to your Formspree inbox for now.
Federal & State Complaints

Turn Anger into Action, Not Just Posts

Most Americans never file a formal complaint because the forms are buried, confusing, or scattered across dozens of sites. America’s Watchlist aims to change that by putting the most important links in one place — and eventually generating pre-filled forms based on the alerts you care about.

Federal Ethics & Misconduct

For issues involving members of Congress, federal judges, or executive-branch officials.

Typical issues:

Ethics violations, corruption, abuse of office, failure to act on serious allegations, or protecting donors and friends instead of the law.

Where this message can go:

House & Senate Ethics Committees, the Department of Justice, Inspector General offices, and your U.S. Representative and Senators.

Demo only: right now, this form sends to your Formspree inbox so you can review, edit, and forward to House/Senate ethics or the DOJ. In a full build, automated tools can route complaints directly to the proper offices.

State-Level Complaints

For governors, state legislators, state courts, and local officials.

Typical issues:

Unfair tax or rate hikes pushed through without transparency, state agencies ignoring court orders or citizens’ rights, or officials using power to punish critics instead of serving the people.

Where to send:

State ethics commissions, inspectors general, attorneys general, and your state house and senate members.

Demo only: these complaints also arrive in your Formspree inbox so you can forward them to state ethics offices, attorneys general, or local leaders. Later, this can be wired with official state contact links for each state.
