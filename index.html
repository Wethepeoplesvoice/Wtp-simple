When Washington stops listening, the Constitution does not leave the people powerless. Article V gives Americans and the states a peaceful, lawful way to demand big reforms ‚Äî but most citizens were never really taught what it means.

How Amendments Can Start
Two Paths, Both Require the People
Article V explains how the Constitution can be amended:
  • By Congress: Two-thirds of both the House and Senate vote to propose an amendment.
  • By the States: Two-thirds of state legislatures (34 states) can call for a convention to propose amendments.
No matter how it starts, any amendment still must be approved by three-fourths of the states to take effect. That means broad national agreement is required ‚Äî no one group can hijack the process alone.

  • Clearer limits on donor and lobbyist influence.
