Government has grown bigger, more complicated, and harder to follow. Most people are working, raising families, and trying to survive — they don’t have hours to dig through 8,000-page bills, budgets, and legal language. That fog is where bad decisions, quiet deals, and unfair policies slip through.

Every year the IRS and state governments collect huge sums from families, workers, and small businesses. States stack on income taxes, sales taxes, property taxes, gas taxes, “fees,” and fines. We fund bloated agencies, useless positions, and contracts with no spending caps — while ordinary Americans live paycheck to paycheck.

At the same time, billionaires, big donors, and media empires pour money into the system to control the story we hear. They keep us divided and distracted while the same insiders stay in office for decades and grow richer off our work.

America’s Torch exists for people who are done being played. It’s for moms, dads, grandparents, and young people who still love this country, who still believe in peaceful change, and who know that if we stand together, no government, party, or network will ever blow out our flame.

This platform is a simple promise: to shine light on what they’re doing, in language everyone can understand, and give millions of ordinary Americans an easy way to act together — instead of shouting alone at a screen.

Hot Right Now If the people who swore an oath will not protect our rights, our money, and our peace, then the people themselves must step in — peacefully, clearly, and in numbers too big to ignore. America’s Torch is one way to do that.
TAKE ACTION

A Peaceful Movement for Accountability

America’s Torch is not another place to argue online. It’s a set of tools for real-world, peaceful pressure: citizens seeing the same facts at the same time and moving together.

1. See What’s Really Happening

In a full build, live dashboards would track bills, votes, rate hikes, and spending in plain English — so you can see when something dangerous is moving before it’s too late to react.

2. Vote on Proposals & Petitions

Citizens can vote on national proposals, sign focused petitions, and help shape which problems we push on first — instead of waiting for party leaders or cable news to tell us what matters.

3. Send Real Complaints, Not Just Posts

When officials cross the line, the platform can help citizens fill out official ethics, oversight, or civil-rights forms and send them to the right committee — federal or state — instead of hunting for links one by one.

4. Pressure Without Violence

No riots, no threats, no doxxing. Just millions of citizens watching the same alerts, signing the same petitions, and sending clear, peaceful messages that can’t be ignored.

In a full build, America’s Torch would plug into bill trackers, campaign finance data, public budgets, and real-time feeds so citizens can see what’s happening in their own state — and across the country — in one place.

Each state gets its own dashboard: bills on the move, new spending, tax changes, and who voted yes or no — all in plain English, not legalese.

  • Bills and resolutions that actually affect households.
  • Major votes with names, not just party lines.

Citizens can see national trends: which issues keep repeating, which states quietly pass copy-cat bills, and where the same special interests appear again and again.

  • Share those patterns with neighbors, family, and friends.

Petition #1

Real Transparency and Accountability in Government

To the elected officials of the United States:

We, the undersigned citizens, are deeply concerned about the growing distance between government power and the people it is supposed to serve. We call for:

  1. Real-time transparency for major federal spending, rate hikes, and new taxes that impact households.
  2. Full disclosure of large donations, financial conflicts, and foreign-linked income for all members of Congress and senior officials, with easy, public tools for citizens to see who is paying whom.
  3. Stronger, faster oversight when officials abuse their office, break the public trust, ignore the will of the people, or protect their friends instead of the law.

These reforms are not about left or right — they are about restoring basic trust, honesty, and accountability in a government that is supposed to belong to the people.

Demo only: for now, your signature goes to your Formspree inbox so you can count and export them later. In a full build, verified U.S.-citizen accounts and direct-delivery tools would send this same message straight to Congress and state leaders.
Petition #2

6-Year Term Limits for Congress

To the United States Congress:

We, the undersigned, believe that public office is a temporary duty, not a lifetime career. When the same politicians stay in power for decades, they become more loyal to donors, lobbyists, and their own status than to the people back home.

We call for a national law that:

  1. Limits members of the House to a total of three terms (6 years).
  2. Limits members of the Senate to a total of one term (6 years).
  3. Prevents any member from using loopholes, appointments, or “musical chairs” jobs to stay in power indefinitely.

Without real term limits, the American people will never fully trust the federal government again. Power will never return to ordinary citizens as long as the same insiders stay in office for life, fueled by big donors and billionaire money.

Demo only: like Petition #1, signatures are stored in your Formspree inbox for now so you can track and export them. In a future build, they can be verified and forwarded automatically to Congress.

Official Complaints & Forms

Turn Anger into Action, Not Just Posts

Most Americans never file a formal complaint because the forms are buried, confusing, or scattered across dozens of sites. America’s Torch aims to change that by putting the most important links in one place — and eventually generating pre-filled forms based on the alerts you care about.
