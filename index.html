National Petition – America’s Watchlist

For Term Limits, Transparency & Accountability

Citizens can sign this petition and the same message can be copied into official contact forms for Congress and state leaders.

Petition Text

To the elected officials of the United States:

We, the undersigned citizens, are deeply concerned about the growing distance between government power and the people it is supposed to serve.

We call for:

  1. Real-time transparency for major federal spending, rate hikes, and new taxes that impact households.
  2. Clear, public disclosure of large donations, gifts, and foreign-linked income for all members of Congress.
  3. National term limits so no one can build a lifelong political empire while ordinary Americans struggle to be heard.
  4. Stronger, faster ethics oversight when officials appear to abuse their office, ignore the will of the people, or undermine trust in our institutions.

We believe these reforms are not about left or right — they are about restoring trust, fairness, and basic accountability. The longer Washington protects itself instead of the people, the harder it will be for faith in our government to be repaired.

We respectfully urge you to act now and put the interests of the American people above party, donors, and personal power.

Sign the Petition
I understand this is a peaceful, non-partisan petition and agree to have my name counted with other citizens asking for these reforms.
After signing, you can copy this petition text into official Congressional contact pages and state portals to multiply the impact.

How It Starts

1. Launch the public site. Share this page as a peaceful, citizen-powered movement — not a party, not a campaign.

2. Collect interest by state. Track where people are signing up so you know which states to focus on first.

3. Wire in real data & logins. A developer can later connect bill-tracking APIs, campaign finance data, and secure authentication.

4. Start peaceful pressure. Use facts, alerts, and numbers — not violence — to put real pressure on people in power.
